\caption{CWT scalograms for a synthetic chirp in isolation and under LOSA time-warping ($\Delta\phi=3$~rad). (a)--(b) Log-power scalograms (relative to peak), computed with a complex Morlet wavelet (cmor6-1); gray indicates the cone of influence. (c) Signed fractional power difference $\Delta P/(P+\epsilon)$ (masked to the signal band); the black curve shows the analytic chirp frequency $f(t)$ (not ridge-extracted); alternating lobes aligned with the chirp ridge arise from the first-order response of the CWT to a small time warp, i.e.\ a derivative-like structure rather than random artifacts. (d) Analytic LOSA time shift $\Delta t(t)=\tfrac{1}{2}(a_{\mathrm{los}}/c)t^2$; dashed curve shows $\Delta f(t)=(\mathrm{d}f/\mathrm{d}t)\,\Delta t(t)$.}
